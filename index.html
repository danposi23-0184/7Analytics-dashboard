Analysis

Key Findings

Observations drawn from the dashboard visuals and data exploration.

01
Cebu Led All Provinces in Total Votes
The bar chart shows Cebu ranking first among all provinces in total vote count, followed closely by Cavite. This reflects both population density and high voter participation in these areas.
02
Go, Bong Go Topped the Candidate Chart
Among all candidates, Bong Go (PDPLBN) received the highest total votes approaching 30 million, followed by Aquino, Bam (KNP) and Dela Rosa, Bato (PDPLBN). All top candidates are nationally known figures.
03
National Races Outpaced Local in Volume
The donut chart reveals that National contest types accounted for roughly 59.96% of total votes (657M), while Local races made up 40.04% (439M). This is expected since national races cover the entire electorate.
04
Map Shows Luzon as the Activity Hotspot
The municipality map highlights dense clusters of voter activity in the Luzon region, with several municipalities marked in red as high-turnout areas. Geographic spread confirms nationwide participation.
05
Average Turnout Metric Needs Revisiting
The Average Turnout shows 3.31%. This means that on average, roughly 3.31% of registered voters per municipality record participated — a figure that reflects precinct-level granularity rather than the total national turnout rate.
06
Slicers Confirm the Model is Working
All three slicers (Province, Municipality, Contest Type) successfully filter every visual on the page, confirming that the one-to-many relationships in the star schema are functioning correctly.
Process

Data Methodology

How the raw COMELEC data was cleaned, modeled, and prepared for analysis.

Data Cleaning — Power Query Editor
01
Removed Blank Rows
Rows with missing candidate names and null vote values were filtered out to prevent inaccurate aggregations and empty card visuals in the dashboard.
02
Checked and Corrected Data Types
Numerical columns (votes, registered_voters, actual_voters, percentage) were set to decimal or whole number types. Text columns were set to Text type to prevent sorting and filter errors.
03
Removed Duplicate Records
Duplicate rows were identified and removed to prevent double-counting of votes or candidate entries during aggregation.
04
Removed the voting_center Column
Dropped as it was too granular for the planned dashboard visuals and not referenced in any metrics.
05
Standardized Text Formatting
Text.Proper was applied to province, municipality, barangay, contest_type, and contest_name to ensure consistent capitalization across all records.
06
Added location_key Column
A custom column combining province and municipality was created in both cleaned_local and location_dim. Formula: [province] & " | " & [municipality]. This resolved the many-to-many relationship issue.

DAX Measures
Total Registered Voters
= SUM(cleaned_local[registered_voters])
Sum of all registered voters across every precinct and location.
Total Actual Voters
= COUNTROW(cleaned_local[actual_voters])
Sum of voters who actually cast their votes on election day.
Average Turnout
= AVERAGE(cleaned_local[percentage]/100)
Average voter turnout percentage across all municipalities.
Total Votes
= SUM(cleaned_local[votes])
Total votes received by all candidates in all contests.
Highest Vote Count
= MAX(cleaned_local[votes])
The highest number of votes received by any single candidate.
Number of Candidates
= DISTINCTCOUNT(cleaned_local[candidate])
Total count of unique candidates across all contest types.
